> A history unknown. An experience not lived. The path unwalked. 
> Visions from another life.

It's not uncommon for the soul of melancholic disposition to look back on his life and wonder wether the cumulative experiences of what he has lived are the zenith or peak of what the human experience can offer. There is always a lingering regret at the seams of our lives, which tends to comprise most of it, of routines, old habits and a mechanic repetition of day-to-day activities. I always had this suspicion that this was a design of the mecanical, machine-like hellish realm we have constructed for ourselves, but enough time and reflection have made me aware that this design permeates the entirety of existence, up to time and history itself.

A defeating & spirit-killing rotten exercise I tended to subjugate myself to in this regard was to fabricate stories and alternate events in my mind as to what the outcome had I made better decisions. Take a risk where none was taken. To dissolve my pride and digest a loss; hardest thing I could ever do. Most times, I realise the futility of this poison and let go. But these phantom histories persist, these quantum branches where different choices were made, hovering at the edge of what could have been.

How we shape our lives and life shapes us. It's certainly interesting how catastrophe and defeat tend to bring the nature of people forward. *For most, it brings out the best in us.*

The relentless determination that defines the human spirit has certainly no ceiling. One may only take a look at our history, the sacrifices of our ancestors, who wielded the heroic spirit against the colossal forces of tyranny and evil. And in this history one finds the solace in the fact that human beings have been confronted and fought the same old demons and ordeals that we know so well.

Our challenges are unique to our time and age, but the nature of the world and the collective madness and dreams of humanity permeates all, both the natural and man-made machinery. In all this, one can forget how history is shaped by every waking minute, and it is not something that only happens to us; it is something we do. Every moment, every choice, collapses infinite possibilities into a single path, leaving all the other histories unwalked, unlived, unknown. If the last 5 years have taught me anything, is that reality bends easily to the will of men.

In the battlefield of time, all those who leave their mark are victors. No victory is final. No defeat is permanent. Both are illusory impostors derived from our short-sightedness. The work we do now will reverberate into the future. It is within our hands, to surpass the most glorious of victories, so that whether you sail or struggle to keep afloat in the river of time, you stand the course to fulfill your mission and promise towards the future. 

Cast out the notion of destiny, it is a mere replacement for the free nature of people and their natural disposition to pursue higher ambitions. That is what most call destiny. It comes to you once you choose to play your own game, and stay determined to embrace it's offering, whatever they may be. All the quantum histories, all the possible paths, converge into the one you make real.

Life is no mistery. Life is fairly simple and brilliant. What we do with ourselves, in the face of our history, legacy and dreams; is the true enigma. What remains unspoken are all the histories we did not live, the selves we did not become, the quantum ghosts of decisions unmade.
